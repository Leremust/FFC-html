Mount Kilimanjaro

The Highest Mountain in Africa

Here are Ten Interesting Facts About Mount Kilimanjaro

  • It is one of the Seven Summits - Mount Kilimanjaro is the tallest mountain in Africa, making it one of the seven summits.
  • Kilimanjaro Stands on its own - Kilimanjaro is not only Africa’s tallest peak, but also the world’s tallest free standing mountain.
  • The Mountain is on the Equator - The equator is an imaginary line that divides the Northern Hemisphere and Southern Hemisphere. Mount Kilimanjaro lies just 205 miles from the equator, in the country of Tanzania.
  • Its Created by three Volcanic Cones - As mentioned above, Kilimanjaro was formed from volcanic activity. However, the mountain once had three volcanic cones – Kibo, Shira and Mawenzi.
  • Kilimanjaro isn't Dead its Dormant - Shira and Mawenzi are extinct volcanoes. However, Kibo is considered a dormant volcano; it can erupt again!
  • The First Ascention was more than a Century ago - Mount Kilimanjaro was first climbed in 1889 by a German geologist Hans Meyer, an Austrian climber Ludwig Purtscheller and a local guide Yohani Kinyala Lauwo.
  • Half of the People who attemp Kilimanjaro Failed - Now approximately 30,000 people climb Kilimanjaro every year. Though not substantiated, it is often reported that 50% of climbers fail.
  • Elite Athlets have Climbed Kilimanjaro Real Fast - Kilimanjaro has been tackled at a pace that will amaze you. The fastest ascent and descent of Kilimanjaro was completed by Swiss Karl Egloff in just 6 hours and 42 minutes in 2014.
  • The Young and the Old have Conqured Kilimanjaro - The oldest person to successfully climb Kilimanjaro is 89 year old American woman Anne Lorimor, while the youngest person to climb Kilimanjaro is American Coaltan Tanner, who summited at age six in 2018.
  • The journey to the summit is like going from Equator to Antarctica - While climbing Kilimanjaro, trekkers will experience five distinct ecological zones on their way to the top. These include: Bushland, Rainforest, Heath, Alpine Desert and Arctic.
